# Judge Q&A Cheatsheet

## What did you build?

We built Miro Supply, a factory-risk simulation demo on top of an existing simulation engine.

It turns a supply-chain shock into:

- a failure chain
- a constrained node
- an estimated disruption window
- recommended actions
- an interrogation / trust moment

## What problem are you solving?

Factories do not need another generic AI dashboard.

They need to know:

- what breaks first
- how urgent the disruption is
- what action buys time

## What is novel here?

Most industrial AI demos are dashboards or copilots.

This demo models a **causal operational cascade** and turns it into an explainable operational brief.

## Is this a forecast?

No.

It is a decision-support scenario simulator.

That is why we use language like:

- estimated disruption window
- constrained node
- mitigation actions

instead of exact certainty claims.

## Why does this fit the hackathon tracks?

### Codex-Powered Services

Codex was the backbone of the build process, orchestration, runtime tooling, and demo system.

### AI Applications

This is an AI-native application for a real industrial problem:

- supply-chain disruption analysis
- factory operations risk
- mitigation prioritization

## What did you contribute that is uniquely yours?

Our contribution is the product layer and demo system:

- factory-risk framing
- synthetic factory scenario and shocks
- knowledge-graph grounding pack
- operational brief workflow
- fallback demo bundle
- reproducible refresh command

## What are the limitations?

- synthetic data
- no ERP / SAP integrations
- hackathon scope
- not a precise forecasting engine

## Why is this useful?

Because operators need a coherent, explainable operational story fast:

- what broke
- why it matters
- what to do now

Instead of reacting to supply shocks with spreadsheets and panic, a factory operator gets a fast, explainable operational risk brief.
