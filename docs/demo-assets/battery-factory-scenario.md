# Demo Scenario: Battery Materials Factory

## Factory

Northstar Battery Components is a mid-sized manufacturer in Nevada that produces lithium-ion battery modules for commercial EV fleets in North America.

## Output

- 12,000 battery modules per month
- primary customers are two EV fleet OEMs
- one assembly line is responsible for 65% of monthly output

## Critical Inputs

- lithium precursor from two Chinese suppliers
- cathode material from a South Korean processor
- separators from one Japanese Tier-1 supplier
- power electronics from a Mexican contract manufacturer

## Operating Assumptions

- 18 days of lithium precursor inventory on hand
- 12 days of separator inventory on hand
- cathode safety stock is low because of cost pressure
- expedited freight is possible but expensive
- customer contracts penalize late delivery after 10 days

## Constraints

- procurement cannot instantly qualify new chemistry suppliers
- the plant cannot swap to an alternate module design this quarter
- one upstream supplier already has quality variability concerns
- port rerouting adds cost and 5-8 days of delay

## Stakeholders To Simulate

- plant manager
- procurement lead
- logistics coordinator
- supplier account manager
- OEM customer operations lead
- regulator / trade-policy observer

## Core Question

If a supply shock hits a critical material flow, what fails first, how quickly does production risk become severe, and what mitigation buys the most time?
